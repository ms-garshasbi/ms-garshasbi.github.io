About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Computer Science at Karlstad University, Sweden, specialising in AI-driven optimisation for edge–cloud environments. During my PhD, in collaboration with Ericsson, I have developed a range of AI-based methods (from heuristic strategies to advanced learning-based models) for adaptive service placement across the edge–cloud continuum. My research investigates the role of AI-driven optimisation in enhancing robust, scalable, and agile decision-making in dynamic, large-scale distributed systems, thereby improving performance, responsiveness, and reliability in real-world conditions.

Research Interests

My research interests include adaptive optimisation, machine learning for distributed systems, and decision-making under uncertainty, particularly in online learning and evolutionary approaches. I am interested in developing practical and scalable solutions that bridge the gap between theoretical research and real-world systems, especially in dynamic environments where continuous adaptation is essential. I also explore emerging directions in AI-driven systems, network automation, and efficient large-scale infrastructures, with a focus on how these technologies can facilitate next-generation intelligent services.
