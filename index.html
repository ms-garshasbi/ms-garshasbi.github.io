Research Overview

Mohammadsadeq Garshasbi Herabad is a PhD student in Computer Science at Karlstad University, Sweden. His research focuses on intelligent service optimisation in edge-cloud environments, with particular emphasis on latency-critical and computationally intensive workloads. In close collaboration with Ericsson, he develops advanced learning-based solutions for optimising service placement across the edge-cloud continuum, aiming to improve system performance, responsiveness, and reliability in dynamic and large-scale infrastructures.

Research Focus & Interests

His research lies at the intersection of edge computing, cloud systems, and intelligent optimisation, with a strong emphasis on decision-making in dynamic and resource-constrained environments. He is particularly interested in designing advanced optimisation frameworks that enable low-latency, reliable, and adaptive service orchestration across the edge-cloud continuum. A core component of his work is the development of lightweight machine-learning and evolutionary optimisation methods for service placement and resource management, targeting latency-critical and computationally intensive applications, such as AR/VR and real-time interactive services. These solutions are designed for online operation, where infrastructure conditions, workloads, and network characteristics continuously evolve. Driven by practical deployment constraints and industry requirements, his research bridges theoretical optimisation techniques with real-world systems, addressing challenges including response-time minimisation, system reliability, scalability, and robustness to failures and environmental drift.
